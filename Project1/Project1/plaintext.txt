portrait of durers father at is an oil on linden panel painting 
attributed to the german painter and printmaker albrecht durer 
although a master goldsmith and well travelled  albrecht durer 
the elder the painters father lived in poverty all his life 
with his much younger wife he fathered children of whom only two reached adulthood 
he was supportive of his sons precocious talent and sent him to an 
apprenticeship with michael wolgemut one of the most highly 
regarded painters in nuremberg at the time 
this portrait was painted in on his sons return but whether it is the 
original work or one of several copies done in the artists workshop is unclear 
the painting currently hangs at the national gallery london
